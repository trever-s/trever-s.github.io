Business Challenge

Prospects weren't converting into customers under current marketing initiatives.

Process

We interviewed our target audience: two-dozen Fortune 500 senior marketers, each placed in different technology companies, and responsible for making purchasing decisions.

As well as interviews, we sent out surveys asking what marketers would do on our website when looking to acquire an agency specializing in CRM and Marketing Automation solutions.

It turned out that most of them had a number of decisions which were scattered, if not impossible, to pinpoint through targeted messaging. However, there were quite a few similarities, even if the language marketers used to describe their needs was varied.

We used our research and developed a detailed persona document, along with customer stories, as a byproduct of what was learned about our target audience. The persona document quickly became a fixture for strategic conversations across the company.

Hiccups

We went overboard and created too many personas. Even though it was a good idea to divide them in half by Decision Makers and Influencers we may have given each persona too much backstory.

Results

We were able to idenitfy and devise a content strategy based around three major concerns marketers expressed when they sought out an agency.

The insight we gathered and content strategy built around it was a success. Within three months we had a 18% increase in new business acquisition through the Homepage, Blog and social media. In six months we had an increase of 26% and growing.

Continual Improvement

To this day we are still monitoring (and responding to) changes in 5 key baseline KPIs we established with Google Analytics and using Marketing Automation technology.
